WHAT'S THE T

What's the T? was a humorous, lighthearted gossip column that ran regularly on BadatSports.com from 2013-15. Predominantly covering the Chicago area, What's the T? also released editions from Miami, Minneapolis, Mexico City and other locations.

EXPO REGISTER

Commissioned for the first edition EXPO Chicago, the EXPO REGISTER was produced and edited by Bassett for Bad at Sports throughout the art fair's duration in September 2013. Designed by Clay Hickson of Tan & Loose Press, the REGISTER was released at the fair as limited edition risograph print featuring 4 different color combinations. Additional contributors include Ross Jordan, Caroline Picard, Jesse Malmed, Anthony Romero, Andrew Santa-Lucia, Sofia Leiby, Vincent Uribe, Isa Giallorenzo and more.

_________________________

ACRE PAPER

The ACRE PAPER was produced and edited by Bassett in collaboration with ACRE and NADA for the 2012 edition of their art fair at the Deauville Hotel in Miami Beach. Designed by Edau, the publication also featured contributions from Domingo Castillo, Monica Uszerowicz, Nicholas Lobo, Nina Hartmann, Jessica Gross, Kevin Arrow, Jim Drain and more.

_________________________

PIER REVIEW

Evolving from the 2013 EXPO REGISTER, the Pier Review was published in collaboration with ArtSlant and THE SEEN (EXPO Chicago) for the 2014 edition of the art fair. The expanded format of the publication enabled the inclusion of long-form essays in addition to art fair coverage. The Review featured a limited edition print by artist José Lerma, and contributions from Caroline Picard, Alyssa Martinez, Jessica Cochran, Shreya Sethi, Thea Liberty Nichols, Ionit Behar, Sid Branca, Gan Uyeda, Britt Julious and many more.

Bio

WRITER | EDITOR | PRODUCER

Dana Bassett is a writer, editor, producer, problem solver, logistical coordinator, fundraiser, shapeshifter, talker, listener and a sensitive, yet demanding, soul. Born in Miami Florida under the Leo moon, Dana currently in the Pilsen neighborhood of Chicago with her husband, sister, their fish and her sister’s cat, Norbu. She has directed organizations such as The Artists’ Cooperative Residency and Exhibitions Project (ACRE), Borscht Film Festival, and is the current Executive Producer of Bad at Sports, the longest running arts podcast (in the world?). Her writing has been published in outlets such as The Smudge, Hyperallergic, The Miami Rail and The Seen. She has also worked with Red Bull, Lumpen Radio and Common Field .
